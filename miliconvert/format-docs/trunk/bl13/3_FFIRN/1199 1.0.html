INTERNATIONAL MARITIME DANGEROUS GOODS INDICATOR (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

CLASS 1.1

1.1

SUBSTANCES AND ARTICLES THAT HAVE A MASS EXPLOSION HAZARD. A MASS EXPLOSION HAZARD IS ONE THAT AFFECTS VIRTUALLY THE ENTIRE LOAD PRACTICALLY INSTANTANEOUSLY.

CLASS 1.2

1.2

SUBSTANCES AND ARTICLES THAT HAVE A PROJECTION HAZARD, BUT NOT A MASS EXPLOSION HAZARD.

CLASS 1.3

1.3

SUBSTANCES AND ARTICLES THAT HAVE A FIRE HAZARD AND EITHER A MINOR BLAST HAZARD OR A MINOR PROJECTION HAZARD OR BOTH, BUT NOT A MASS EXPLOSION HAZARD. INCLUDES ITEMS THAT GENERATE CONSIDERABLE RADIANT HEAT OR BURN ONE AFTER THE OTHER, PRODUCING MINOR BLAST OR PROJECTION EFFECTS OR BOTH.

CLASS 1.4

1.4

SUBSTANCES AND ARTICLES THAT PRESENT NO SIGNIFICANT HAZARD. EFFECTS ARE LARGELY CONFINED TO THE PACKAGE AND NO PROJECTION OF FRAGMENTS OF APPRECIABLE SIZE OR RANGE ARE TO BE EXPECTED. AN EXTERNAL FIRE MUST NOT CAUSE PRACTICALLY INSTANTANEOUS EXPLOSION OF VIRTUALLY THE ENTIRE CONTENTS OF THE PACKAGE.

CLASS 1.5

1.5

VERY INSENSITIVE SUBSTANCES THAT HAVE A MASS EXPLOSION HAZARD. INCLUDES EXPLOSIVE SUBSTANCES THAT ARE SO INSENSITIVE THAT THERE IS VERY LITTLE PROBABILITY OF INITIATION OF, OR TRANSITION FROM BURNING TO DETONATION UNDER NORMAL CONDITIONS OF TRANSPORT. AS A MINIMUM THEY MUST NOT EXPLODE IN THE EXTERNAL FIRE TEST.

CLASS 2.1

2.1

FLAMMABLE GASES

CLASS 2.2

2.2

NONFLAMMABLE GASES

CLASS 2.3

2.3

POISONOUS GASES NOTE: POISONOUS GASES THAT ARE ALSO FLAMMABLE SHOULD BE SEGREGATED AS CLASS 2.1 GASES.

CLASS 3.1

3.1

LOW FLASHPOINT LIQUIDS HAVING A FLASHPOINT BELOW MINUS 18 DEGREES CENTIGRADE (ZERO (O) DEGREES FAHRENHEIT), CLOSED CUP TEST.

CLASS 3.2

3.2

INTERMEDIATE FLASHPOINT GROUP OF LI

 

QUIDS HAVING A FLASHPOINT OF MINUS 18 DEGREES CENTIGRADE (ZERO (0) DEGREES FAHRENHEIT) UP TO, BUT NOT INCLUDING 23 DEGREES CENTIGRADE (73 DEGREES FAHRENHEIT), CLOSED CUP TEST.

CLASS 3.3

3.3

HIGH FLASHPOINT GROUP OF LIQUIDS HAVING A FLASHPOINT OF 23 DEGREES CENTIGRADE (73 DEGREES FAHRENHEIT) UP TO, AND INCLUDING, 61 DEGREES CENTIGRADE (141 DEGREES FAHRENHEIT), CLOSED CUP TEST.

CLASS 4.1-FLAMMABLE SOLIDS

4.1

SOLIDS POSSESSING THE PROPERTIES OF BEING EASILY IGNITED BY EXTERNAL SOURCES SUCH AS SPARKS AND FLAMES, AND OF BEING READILY COMBUSTIBLE, OR OF BEING LIABLE TO CAUSE OR CONTRIBUTE TO FIRE THROUGH FRICTION.

CLASS 4.2-SPONTANEOUS COMBUSTIBLES

4.2

SOLIDS OR LIQUIDS POSSESSING THE PROPERTY OF BEING LIABLE, SPONTANEOUSLY, TO HEAT AND TO IGNITE.

CLASS 4.3-EMITTERS OF FLAMMABLE GASES WHEN WET

4.3

SOLIDS OR LIQUIDS POSSESSING THE PROPERTY, WHEN IN CONTACT WITH WATER, OF EMITTING FLAMMABLE GASES. IN SOME CASES, THE GASES ARE LIABLE TO SPONTANEOUS IGNITION.

CLASS 5.1-OXIDIZING AGENTS

5.1

SUBSTANCES THAT, ALTHOUGH IN THEMSELVES ARE NOT NECESSARILY COMBUSTIBLE, MAY EITHER BY YIELDING OXYGEN OR BY SIMILAR PROCESSES, INCREASE THE RISK AND INTENSITY OF FIRE IN OTHER MATERIALS WITH WHICH THEY COME INTO CONTACT.

CLASS 5.2-ORGANIC PEROXIDES

5.2

SUBSTANCES THAT CONTAIN THE BIVALENT -0-0- STRUCTURE AND MAY BE CONSIDERED DERIVATIVES OF HYDROGEN PEROXIDE, WHERE ONE OR BOTH OF THE HYDROGEN ATOMS HAVE BEEN REPLACED BY ORGANIC RADICALS. ORGANIC PEROXIDES ARE THERMALLY UNSTABLE SUBSTANCES THAT MAY UNDERGO EXOTHERMIC SELF-ACCELERATING DECOMPOSITION. IN ADDITION, THEY MAY HAVE ONE OR MORE OF THE FOLLOWING PROPERTIES: LIABLE TO EXPLOSIVE DECOMPOSITION; BURN RAPIDLY; SENSITIVE TO IMPACT OR FRICTION; REACT DANGEROUSLY WITH OTHER SUBSTANCES; AND/OR CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE EYES.

CLASS 6.1-POISONOUS (TOXIC) SUBSTANCES

6.1

SUBSTANCES LIABLE TO EITHER CAUSE DEATH OR SERIOUS INJURY OR TO HARM HUMAN HEALTH IF SWALLOWED OR INHALED, OR BY SKIN CONTACT.

CLASS 6.2-INFECTIOUS SUBSTANCES

6.2

SUBSTANCES CONTAINING DISEASE PRODUCING MICRO-ORGANISMS.
