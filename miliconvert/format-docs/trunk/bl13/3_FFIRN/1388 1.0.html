Table 1388/1 - CLOUD TYPE (2-2)

 

Related Documents:

None

 

Sponsor:

None

 

Unit of Measure:

 

 

CLOUD TYPE (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

CIRRUS

CI

A WHITE CLOUD OF MINUTE ICE CRYSTALS FORMED AT ALTITUDES OF 20,000 TO 40,000 FT.

CIRROCUMULUS

CC

A CLOUD FORM OF SMALL ROUNDED MASSES AT HIGH ALTITUDE, USUALLY IN REGULAR GROUPINGS, FORMING A MACKEREL SKY.

CIRROSTRATUS

CS

A UNIFORM LAYER OF HIGH STRATUS CLOUD FORM.

ALTOCUMULUS

AC

A FLEECY CLOUD FORMATION CONSISTING OF LARGE WHITISH GLOBULAR CLOUDLETS WITH SHADED PORTIONS.

ALTOSTRATUS

AS

A CLOUD FORMATION SIMILAR TO CIRROSTRATUS BUT DARKER AND AT A LOWER LEVEL.

NIMBOSTRATUS

NS

A LOW DARK GRAY RAINY CLOUD LAYER.

STRATOCUMULUS

SC

ROLLS OF DARK CLOUD WHICH OFTEN COVER THE ENTIRE SKY, ESPECIALLY IN WINTER.

STRATUS

ST

A CLOUD FORM OF GREATER HORIZONTAL EXTENSION AND COMPARATIVELY LOWER ALTITUDE (2000 TO 7000 FT) THAN THE CUMULOSTRATUS OR CIRROSTRATUS.

CUMULUS

CU

A MASSIVE CLOUD FORM HAVING A FLAT BASE AND ROUNDED OUTLINES.

CUMULONIMBUS

CB

A CUMULUS CLOUD SPREAD OUT IN THE SHAPE OF AN ANVIL EXTENDING TO GREAT HEIGHTS.
