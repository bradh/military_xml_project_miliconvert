WEATHER, STATE OF (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

BLOWING DRIZZLE

BLOW-DRIZ

UNIFORM PRECIPITATION COMPOSED EXCLUSIVELY OF FINE DROPS VERY CLOSE TOGETHER BEING BLOWN FROM THE EARTH'S SURFACE.

BLOWING DUST

BLOW-DUST

DUST RAISED BY THE WIND TO MODERATE OR GREAT HEIGHTS ABOVE THE GROUND AND RESTRICTING HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS.

BLOWING SAND

BLOW-SAND

SAND RAISED BY THE WIND TO MODERATE OR GREAT HEIGHTS ABOVE THE GROUND AND RESTRICTING HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS.

BLOWING SNOW

BLOW-SNOW

SNOW PARTICLES RAISED AND STIRRED VIOLENTLY BY THE WIND TO MODERATE OR GREAT HEIGHTS. VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS AND THE SKY MAY BECOME OBSCURED WHEN THE PARTICLES ARE RAISED TO GREAT HEIGHTS.

CLEAR

CLEAR

THE ABSENCE OF LAYERS OF CLOUDS OR OTHER OBSCURING PHENOMENA.

CLOUDY

CLOUDY

SELF-EXPLANATORY.

COLD FRONT

COLD FRONT

A DISCONTINUITY IN THE ANALYZED FIELD OF TEMPERATURE, HUMIDITY OR WINDS WHICH IS GENERALLY CHARACTERIZED BY PREDOMINANTLY COLD AIR REPLACING WARMER AIR.

TROPICAL CYCLONE

CYCLONE

THE GENERAL TERM FOR A CYCLONE THAT ORIGINATES OVER THE TROPICAL OCEANS. AT MATURITY, THE TROPICAL CYCLONE IS ONE OF THE MOST INTENSE AND FEARED STORMS OF THE WORLD.

DRIFTING SNOW

DRFTGSNOW

SNOW PARTICLE RAISED BY WIND TO SMALL HEIGHTS ABOVE THE GROUND. VISIBILITY IS NOT REDUCED BELOW 7 STATUTE MILES (11KM) AT EYE LEVEL (6 FEET OR 1.8M) ALTHOUGH OBSTRUCTION BELOW THIS LEVEL MAY BE VEILED OR HIDDEN BY THE PARTICLES MOVING NEARLY HORIZONTAL TO THE GROUND.

DRIZZLE

DRIZZLE

FAIRLY UNIFORM PRECIPITATION COMPOSED EXCLUSIVELY OF FINE DROPS VERY CLOSE TOGETHER. DRIZZLE APPEARS TO FLOAT WHILE FOLLOWING AIR CURRENTS, ALTHOUGH IT FALLS TO THE GROUND. DRIZZLE DROPS ARE TOO SMALL TO DISTURB, APPRECIABLY. STILL WATER PUDDLES.

DRIZZLE LAST HOUR

DRZLLSTHR

DRIZZLE AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION LOCATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF OBSERVATION.

DRIZZLE AND RAIN

DRZLRAIN

PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES COMPOSED OF BOTH LARGE AND SMALL DROPS.

DUST

DUST

FINE PARTICLES SUSPENDED IN THE AIR WHICH GIVE A TAN OR GRAY TINGE TO DISTANT OBJECTS. THE SUN'S DISK IS PALE AND COLORLESS OR HAS A YELLOW TINGE THROUGH DUST.

DUST STORM

DUSTSTORM

SAME AS BLOWING DUST EXCEPT THAT VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO LESS THAN 5/8 STATUTE MILES (1000 METRES) BUT NOT LESS THAN 5/16 STATUTE MILES (500 METRES).

FOG

FOG

A SUSPENSION OF MINUTE WATER DROPLETS IN THE AIR GENERALLY REDUCING THE HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY AT THE EARTH'S SURFACE. FOR AIRWAYS OBSERVATIONS, HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS AND THE VERTICAL DEPTH OF THE PHENOMENON IS APPROXIMATELY 20 FEET (6 METRES OR GREATER. FOR METAR OBSERVATIONS, THE HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO LESS THAN 5/8 STATUTE MILE (1,000 METRES).

FOG LAST HOUR

FOGLSTHR

FOG AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF OBSERVATION.

FREEZING DRIZZLE

FREEZ-DRIZ

DRIZZLE WHICH FREEZES UPON IMPACT WITH THE GROUND OR WITH OBJECTS IN FLIGHT OR ON THE GROUND.

FREEZING RAIN

FREEZ-RAIN

RAIN WHICH FREEZES UPON IMPACT WITH THE GROUND OR WITH OBJECTS IN FLIGHT OR ON THE GROUND.

FUNNEL CLOUD

FUNNEL-CLD

A VIOLENT, ROTATING COLUMN OF AIR PROTRUDING FROM A CUMULONIMBUS WHICH DOES NOT TOUCH THE SURFACE.

GROUND FOG

GROUNDFOG

THE FOG CONDITION IN WHICH HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS AND VERTICAL DEPTH IS LESS THAN 20 FEET (6 METRES). THIS IS A LOCAL PHENOMENON, OFTEN PATCHY, FORMING FIRST IN LOW LYING AREAS.

HAIL

HAIL

PRECIPITATION OF SMALL BALLS OR OTHER PIECES OF ICE FALLING SEPARATELY OR FROZEN TOGETHER IN IRREGULAR LUMPS. HAILSTONES CONSIST OF ALTERNATE OPAQUE AND CLEAR LAYERS OF ICE IN MOST CASES.

HAIL SHOWER

HAILSHWR

HAIL WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

HAZE

HAZE

A SUSPENSION IN THE AIR OF EXTREMELY SMALL, DRY PARTICLES INVISIBLE TO THE NAKED EYE AND SUFFICIENTLY NUMEROUS TO GIVE THE AIR AN OPALESCENT APPEARANCE. THE PHENOMENON RESEMBLES A UNIFORM VEIL OVER THE LANDSCAPE THAT SUBDUES ALL COLORS.

HEAVY RAIN

HEAVYRAIN

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES. RAIN SEEMINGLY FALLS IN SHEETS AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY SNOW

HEAVYSNOW

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS FALLING AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HIGH WINDS

HIGH-WINDS

WIND VELOCITIES WHICH CONSISTENTLY EXCEED 35 KNOTS OVER AN EXTENDED PERIOD OF TIME.

HURRICANE

HURRICANE

A SEVERE TROPICAL CYCLONE IN THE NORTH ATLANTIC OCEAN, CARIBBEAN SEA, GULF OF MEXICO AND IN THE EASTERN NORTH PACIFIC OF THE WEST COAST OF MEXICO.

HEAVY DRIZZLE AND RAIN

HVYDRZLRN

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES COMPOSED OF BOTH LARGER AND SMALLER DROPS WHERE TOTAL RAINFALL IS FALLING AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY FREEZING DRIZZLE

HVYFZDRZL

INTENSE FAIRLY UNIFORM PRECIPITATION COMPOSED OF FINE DROPS WHICH FREEZE UPON IMPACT WITH THE GROUND OR WITH OBJECTS IN FLIGHT OR ON THE GROUND, FALLING AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.02 INCHES (0.5MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY FREEZING RAIN

HVYFZRAIN

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES WHICH FREEZE UPON IMPACT WITH THE GROUND OR WITH OBJECTS IN FLIGHT OR ON THE GROUND, FALLING AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY RAIN SHOWER

HVYRNSHWR

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES WHICH STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY AND FALLS AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY RAIN AND SNOW

HVYRNSNO

INTENSE PRECIPITATION COMPOSED OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES MIXED WITH ICE CRYSTALS FALLING AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY SNOW SHOWER

HVYSNSHWR

INTENSE PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS WHICH STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY AND FALLS AT A RATE GREATER THAN 0.30 INCHES (7.6MM) PER HOUR.

HEAVY THUNDERSTORM

HVYTSTM

ONE OR MORE SUDDEN ELECTRICAL DISCHARGES, MANIFESTED BY A FLASH OF LIGHT (LIGHTNING) AND A SHARP OR RUMBLING SOUND (THUNDER). IT IS A LOCAL STORM ASSOCIATED WITH A CUMULONIMBUS CLOUD AND ACCOMPANIED BY HEAVY RAIN AND STRONG GUSTS.

HEAVY THUNDERSTORM WITH HAIL

HVYTSTMH

SAME AS HEAVY THUNDERSTORMS WITH THE ADDITION OF HAIL FALLING IN THE LOCAL AREA.

ICE PELLETS

ICEPELLETS

PRECIPITATION OF TRANSPARENT OR TRANSLUCENT PELLETS OF ICE, WHICH ARE ROUND OR IRREGULAR, RARELY CONICAL, AND WHICH HAVE A DIAMETER OF 0.2 INCHES (5MM) OR LESS. THEY MAY BE HARD GRAINS OF ICE CONSISTING OF FROZEN RAINDROPS (SLEET) OR PELLETS OF SNOW ENCASED IN A THIN LAYER OF ICE.

ICE PELLETS LAST HOUR

ICEPLSTHR

ICE PELLETS AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION LOCATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF OBSERVATION.

ICING

ICING

ANY DEPOSIT OR COATING OF ICE ON AN OBJECT, CAUSED BY THE IMPINGEMENT AND FREEZING OF LIQUID HYDROMETERS.

LIGHTNING

LIGHTNING

A LUMINOUS MANIFESTATION ACCOMPANYING A SUDDEN ELECTRICAL DISCHARGE WHICH TAKES PLACE FROM OR INSIDE A CLOUD OR, LESS OFTEN, FROM HIGH STRUCTURES ON THE GROUND OR FROM MOUNTAINS.

LIGHT RAIN

LIGHTRAIN

PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES GENERALLY FALLING AT A RATE OF A TRACE UP TO 0.10 INCHES (2.5 MM) PER HOUR AND COMPOSED OF EITHER DROPS LARGER THAN 0.02 INCHES (0.5MM) OR SMALLER DROPS WHICH ARE WIDELY SEPARATED.

LIGHT SNOW

LIGHTSNOW

PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS, MOSTLY BRANCHED IN THE FORM OF SIX POINTED STARS FALLING AT A RATE LESS THAN 0.10 INCHES (2.5MM) PER HOUR.

LIGHT RAIN SHOWER

LTRNSHWR

LIGHT RAIN WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

LIGHT SNOW SHOWER

LTSNSHWR

LIGHT SNOW WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

MODERATE RAIN

MDTRAIN

PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES GENERALLY FALLING AT A RATE BETWEEN 0.11 AND 0.30 INCHES (2.6 TO 7.6MM) PER HOUR AND COMPOSED OF EITHER DROPS LARGER THAN 0.02 INCHES (0.5 MM) OR SMALLER DROPS WHICH ARE WIDELY SEPARATED.

MODERATE SNOW

MDTSNOW

PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS, MOSTLY BRANCHED IN THE FORM OF SIX POINTED STARS FALLING AT A RATE BETWEEN 0.11 TO 0.30 INCHES (2.6 TO 7.6MM) PER HOUR.

MODERATE THUNDERSTORM

MDTTSTM

THUNDERSTORM WITH PRECIPITATION FALLING AT A RATE BETWEEN 0.11 INCHES AND 0.30 INCHES (2.6 AND 7.6MM) PER HOUR.

MIST

MIST

THE FOG CONDITION IN WHICH HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY IS REDUCED TO 6 STATUTE MILES (10KM) OR LESS BUT NOT LESS THAN 5/8 STATUTE MILES (1,000M). WATER DROPLETS MAY OR MAY NOT BE FREEZING UPON CONTACT WITH EXPOSED OBJECTS.

MOSTLY CLEAR

MOST CLEAR

0.1-0.3 OF OPAQUE CLOUDS.

MOSTLY CLOUDY

MOST CLOUD

0.7-0.9 OF OPAQUE CLOUDS.

NOT SIGNIFICANT

NOSIG

LESS THAN 0.1 OF OPAQUE CLOUDS.

OCCLUDED FRONT

OCLD-FRONT

A COMPOSITE OF TWO FRONTS, FORMED AS A COLD FRONT OVERTAKES A WARM OR STATIONARY FRONT.

OTHER

OTR

None

PARTLY CLEAR

PART CLEAR

0.3-0.7 OF OPAQUE CLOUDS.

RAIN

RAIN

PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES, EITHER IN THE FORM OF DROPS LARGER THAN 0.02 INCHES (0.5MM) OR SMALLER DROPS, WHICH IN CONTRAST TO DRIZZLE, ARE WIDELY SEPARATED.

RAIN LAST HOUR

RAINLSTHR

RAIN AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION LOCATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF THE OBSERVATION.

RAIN SHOWER

RAINSHWR

PRECIPITATION OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

RAIN AND SNOW

RAINSNOW

PRECIPITATION COMPOSED OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES MIXED WITH ICE CRYSTALS.

RAIN AND SNOW SHOWER

RNSNOSHWR

PRECIPITATION COMPOSED OF LIQUID WATER PARTICLES MIXED WITH ICE CRYSTALS WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

SAND STORM

SANDSTORM

SAND RAISED BY THE WIND TO MODERATE OR GREAT HEIGHTS ABOVE THE GROUND AND RESTRICTING VISIBILITY TO LESS THAN 5/8 STATUTE MILES (1,000 METRES) BUT NOT LESS THAN 5/16 STATUTE MILES (500 METRES) (WHICH IS A SEVERE SANDSTORM).

SHALLOW FOG

SHALLOWFOG

A SUSPENSION OF MINUTE WATER DROPLETS (OR ICE CRYSTALS) IN THE AIR WHICH DO NOT REDUCE HORIZONTAL VISIBILITY ABOVE A HEIGHT OF 6 FEET (1.8 METRES). THIS CONDITION MAY ALSO BE REFERRED TO AS "SHALLOW GROUND FOG."

SMOKE

SMOKE

None

SNOW

SNOW

PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS, MOSTLY BRANCHED IN THE FORM OF SIXPOINTED STARS. AT TEMPERATURES HIGHER THAN ABOUT 23 DEGREES FAHRENHEIT (MINUS FIVE DEGREES CENTIGRADE), THE CRYSTALS ARE GENERALLY CLUSTERED TO FORM SNOWFLAKES.

SNOW LAST HOUR

SNOWLSTHR

SNOW AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION LOCATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF OBSERVATION.

SNOW SHOWER

SNOWSHWR

PRECIPITATION OF ICE CRYSTALS WHICH CHANGES INTENSITY OR STARTS AND STOPS ABRUPTLY.

SQUALL LINE

SQUALLINE

ANY NON-FRONTAL OR NARROW BAND OF ACTIVE THUNDERSTORMS (WITH OR WITHOUT SQUALLS).

SQUALLS

SQUALLS

A STRONG WIND CHARACTERIZED BY A SUDDEN ONSET, A DURATION IN THE ORDER OF MINUTES, AND A RATHER SUDDEN DECREASE IN SPEED.

STATIONARY FRONT

STAT-FRONT

A FRONT WHICH IS MOVING AT A SPEED LESS THAN FIVE KNOTS.

SEVERE STORM

SVRSTM

None

TORNADO

TORNADO

A VIOLENT ROTATING COLUMN OF AIR, FORMING A PENDANT, PROTRUDING FROM A CUMULONIMBUS CLOUD, AND TOUCHING THE GROUND. IT NEARLY ALWAYS STARTS AS A FUNNEL CLOUD AND IS ACCOMPANIED BY A LOUD, ROARING NOISE.

TROUGH LINE

TROFLINE

THE AXIS OF AN ELONGATED AREA OF RELATIVELY LOW ATMOSPHERIC PRESSURE.

TROPICAL STORM

TROP-STORM

A TROPICAL CYCLONE WHOSE WINDS RANGE BETWEEN 35 AND 64 KNOTS.

TROPICAL DEPRESSION

TROPDEPRN

A TROPICAL CYCLONE WHOSE WINDS DO NOT EXCEED 34 KNOTS.

THUNDERSTORM VICINTIY

TS VCNTY

DESIGNATES WEATHER SUCH AS THUNDERSTORMS OCCURING WITHIN 25 MILES OF A LOCATION.

THUNDERSTORMS

TSTM

ONE OR MORE SUDDEN ELECTRICAL DISCHARGES, MANIFESTED BY A FLASH OF LIGHT (LIGHTNING) AND A SHARP OR RUMBLING SOUND (THUNDER).

THUNDERSTORM WITH HAIL

TSTMHAIL

A THUNDERSTORM ACCOMPANIED BY HAIL.

THUNDERSTORM LAST HOUR

TSTMLSTHR

THUNDERSTORM OBSERVED AT THE STATION OR OBSERVATION LOCATION DURING THE PRECEDING HOUR BUT NOT AT THE TIME OF OBSERVATION.

TURBULENCE

TURB

A STATE OF FLUID FLOW IN WHICH THE INSTANTANEOUS VELOCITIES EXHIBIT IRREGULAR AND APPARENTLY RANDOM FLUCTUATIONS WHICH AFFECT THE FLIGHT OF AIRCRAFT.

TYPHOON

TYPHOON

A TROPICAL CYCLONE WHOSE WINDS EXCEED 65 KNOTS.

VARIABLE WINDS

VRBW

None

WARM FRONT

WARM FRONT

ANY NON-OCCLUDED FRONT, OR PORTION THEREOF, WHICH MOVES IN SUCH A WAY THAT WARMER AIR REPLACES COLDER AIR.

WATER SPOUT

WATERSPOUT

A VIOLENT ROTATING COLUMN OF AIR, PROTRUDING FROM A CUMULONIMBUS CLOUD, OCCURRING OVER A LARGE BODY OF WATER SUCH AS A BAY, GULF, OR LAKE AND TOUCHING THE WATER SURFACE.
