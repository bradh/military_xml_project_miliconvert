Data Code

Explanation

ACOUSTIC SIMULATION OF SURFACE AND SUBSURFACE FORCES

ACSIMSU

THE IMITATION OR CONTROL OF A SHIP SIGNATURE TO MISLEAD OR DECEIVE THE ENEMY.

AMPHIBIOUS WARFARE

AMPHIBWAR

THE SIMULATION OF SOUNDS OR NOISES THAT RESEMBLE AN AMPHIBIOUS OPERATION, SUCH AS THE DROPPING OF ANCHORS, STARTING BOAT ENGINES, ETC.

BLANKET

BLANKET

EMPLOYED TO OFFSET UNFRIENDLY RADAR DETECTION CAPABILITY BY DISPENSING A BLANKET OF CHAFF OVER A DESIGNATED AREA.

CONTROLLED BREACHES OF COMMUNICATION SECURITY

COMSEC

THE ACT OF CONVEYING THROUGH FRIENDLY COMMUNICATIONS FALSE OR MISLEADING INFORMATION TO SUPPORT AN OVERALL COVER AND DECEPTION PLAN.

CONCEALMENT OF FORCES

CONCFOR

MASK UNAVOIDABLE OPERATIONAL NOISES BY TRANSMITTING APPROPRIATE SOUNDS.

CORNER REFLECTORS

CORNREFL

FLAT REFLECTING SURFACES, OF VARIOUS SHAPES AND SIZES, CONNECTED TO FORM A THREE DIMENSIONAL REFLECTOR.

CORRIDOR

CORRIDOR

TO OFFSET UNFRIENDLY RADAR DETECTION CAPABILITY BY DISPENSING A HEAVY CONTINUOUS TRAIL OF CHAFF FROM AN AIRCRAFT IN A CORRIDOR FASHION AS IT APPROACHES THE RADAR.

CRYPTOGRAPHIC INTRUSION

CRYPT

ACCESS INTO ENEMY ENCRYPTED COMMUNICATIONS SYSTEMS TO INTRODUCE BOGUS MESSAGES.

DECEPTIVE JAMMING

DECJAM

TO PREVENT OR REDUCE THE USE OF THE ENEMY COMMUNICATIONS PORTION OF THE ELECTROMAGNETIC SPECTRUM, AND WITHOUT THE ENEMY BEING AWARE OF THE SOURCE OF EMISSIONS.

ELECTRONIC COVER

ELCOV

THE VARIANCE OF LOCATION AND USAGE OF EMITTERS IN TACTICAL UNITS TO ALTER THE STEREOTYPE PATTERN ASSOCIATED WITH THOSE ELECTROMAGNETIC SIGNATURES.

FALSE ACTIVITY

FACTIVITY

THE INCREASE OR DECREASE OF FRIENDLY ACTIVITY.

FALSE PEAKS

FALPK

THE REVERSAL OF FALSE TRAFFIC LEVELS WHICH SIMULATES UNUSUAL ACTIVITY ON A COMMUNICATION NET.

FALSE TRAFFIC LEVELS

FALTFC

APPLIED TO A COMMUNICATIONS NET OVER A PERIOD OF DAYS TO CREATE THE IMPRESSION OF NORMAL TRAFFIC.

GULL

GULL

THE USE OF A FLOATING RADAR REFLECTOR TO SIMULATE SURFACE TARGETS.

INVERSE GAIN MODULATION

IGMOD

THE METHOD OF BREAKING A LOCK ON A CONICAL SCAN TRACKING RADAR BY PROVIDING FALSE AZIMUTH INFORMATION.

BARRAGE JAMMING

JAMBAR

THE SIMULTANEOUS JAMMING OF A NUMBER OF ADJACENT CHANNELS OR FREQUENCIES.

COMMUNICATIONS NOISE JAMMING

JAMNOISE

THE BROADCAST OR REBROADCAST OF ANY TYPE OF NOISE OR OTHER TRANSMISSION INTENDED TO RENDER THE VICTIM COMMUNICATIONS FREQUENCY UNUSABLE.

REPEATER JAMMING

JAMRPTR

THE INTERCEPT OF THE INTENDED VICTIM SIGNAL, WHICH IS THEN ALTERED AND RETRANSMITTED FOR THE PURPOSE OF FALSIFYING OR DISRUPTING INFORMATION FLOW.

SPOT JAMMING

JAMSPOT

THE JAMMING OF A SPECIFIC CHANNEL OR FREQUENCY.

SWEEP JAMMING

JAMSWEEP

USED TO SEARCH FOR AND TO JAM SIGNALS AUTOMATICALLY OVER A BROAD FREQUENCY BAND, EMPLOYING A SWEEP TECHNIQUE.

SWEEP LOCK-ON JAMMING

JAMSWPLO

THE EMPLOYMENT OF A SWEEPING RECEIVER WITH A JAMMER THAT LOCKS ON A DETECTED VICTIM SIGNAL AND PERFORMS SPOT JAMMING.

TRANSPONDER JAMMING

JAMTRANSP

PROGRAMMED TO RESPOND TO A SPECIFIC TYPE OF VICTIM SIGNAL, WHICH TRIGGERS THE TRANSMISSION OF A PREDETERMINED SIGNAL.

MULTIPLE FALSE TARGETS

MLTFLSTGT

USED TO GENERATE MULTIPLE FALSE TARGETS.

NUISANCE INTRUSION

NUIS

ENTRY INTO ENEMY COMMUNICATIONS NET AS A BONAFIDE SUBSCRIBER, TO HARASS AND DISTRACT THE ENEMY.

PADDING

PADDING

THE INSERTION OF DUMMY CODE OR CYPHER GROUPS INTO INDIVIDUAL VALID ENCRYPTED MESSAGES.

PLANNED MESSAGE INTRUSION

PLMSG

ENTRY OF VALID TRAFFIC INTO THE ENEMY COMMUNICATION NETWORK.

ROUTING

ROUTING

THE CONCEALMENT OF TRAFFIC ROUTING BY SENDING TO STATIONS OTHER THAN THE INTENDED RECIPIENTS.

SIMULATION OF FORCE

SIMFOR

ACHIEVED BY PROJECTING SELECTED AUDIO SIGNALS OR BY EMITTING SOUNDS FROM SPECIFIC AREAS.

UNIT SIMULATION

SIMULAT

THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A NETWORK OF COMMUNICATIONS AND NON-COMMUNICATIONS EMITTERS TO SIMULATE A UNIT OR ORGANISATION.

FALSE TARGET GENERATION OR SPOOFING

SPOOF

DECEPTION OF THE ENEMY BY CREATING FALSE TARGETS.

SELF-PROTECTION CHAFF

SPTCHF

EMPLOYED TO OFFSET UNFRIENDLY RADAR DETECTION AND ASSOCIATED WEAPONS EMPLOYMENT BY DISPENSING SINGULAR OR LIMITED BURSTS OF CHAFF.

SCAN RATE MODULATION

SRMOD

THIS TECHNIQUE CAUSES THE ENEMY RADAR ANTENNA TO LOSE LOCK ON TARGET.

INCDRGPO

INCDSPOOF

A METHOD OF DECEIVING THE ENEMY BY CREATING FALSE TARGETS.

INCDSRMOD

ELECTRONIC COVER

MCDELCOV

THE VARIANCE OF LOCATION AND USAGE OF EMITTERS IN TACTICAL UNITS TO ALTER THE STEREOTYPE PATTERNS ASSOCIATED WITH THOSE ELECTROMAGNETIC SIGNATURES.

FALSE PEAKS

MCDFALPK

THIS TECHNIQUE IS THE REVERSE OF FALSE TRAFFIC LEVELS AND SERVES THE PURPOSE OF SIMULATING UNUSUAL ACTIVITY ON A COMMUNICATION NET.

FALSE TRAFFIC LEVELS

MCDFALTFC

THIS TECHNIQUE IS APPLIED ON A COMMUNICATIONS NET OVER A PERIOD OF DAYS TO CREATE THE IMPRESSION OF NORMAL OPERATIONS.

ROUTING

MCDSIM

ESTABLISHING A NETWORK OF COMMUNICATIONS AND NON-COMMUNICATIONS EMITTERS TO SIMULATE A UNIT OR ORGANISATION. THE CREATION OF FICTITIOUS FRIENDLY UNITS WITH ACTUAL OR SPECIALLY DESIGNED EQUIPMENT.

MULTIPLE FALSE TARGETS

MLTFLSTGT

USED TO GENERATE MULTIPLE FALSE TARGETS.

FALSE ACTIVITY

MNCDACTY

THE INCREASE OR DECREASE OF FRIENDLY ACTIVITY.

SELF-PROTECTION

PROTECT

A METHOD OF TRANSMITTING RADIATION TO PREVENT ENEMY DETECTION.

AMPHIBIOUS WARFARE

SDAAMPHIB

SIMULATE SOUNDS OR NOISES THAT RESEMBLE AN AMPHIBIOUS OPERATION SUCH AS DROPPING ANCHORS, STARTING BOAT ENGINES, ETC.

CONCEALMENT OF FORCES

SDACONC

RIVERINE WARFARE

SDARIVER

SIMULATE RIVER PATROL OPERATIONS.

SIMULATION OF FORCE

SDASIM

THIS TECHNIQUE CAN BE ACHIEVED BY PROJECTING SELECTED AUDIO SIGNALS OR BY EMITTING SOUNDS FROM SPECIFIC AREAS.

SIMULATED TACTICAL MOVEMENTS

SDASIMTAC

ACOUSTIC SIMULATION OF SURFACE AND SUBSURFACE FORCES

SDUACSIM

PILL

SDUPILL
