Table 1237/1 - TYPE OF GRAPHIC SHAPE (3-6)

 

Related Documents:

Unit of Measure:

 

 

TYPE OF GRAPHIC SHAPE (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

AREA

AREA

SEQUENCE OF GRID REF DESCRIBES AN AREA CLOCKWISE; THE FIRST FIELD MAY NOT BE REPEATED AT THE END TO CLOSE THE LINE.

ARROW

ARROW

THE FIRST GRID REF DESCRIBES THE BEGINNING (ARROW HEAD OR POINT) AND THE SECOND GRID REF DESCRIBES THE END OF THE ARROW.

ELLIPSE

ELL

THE FIRST REF DESCRIBES THE CENTER OF THE ELLIPSE; THE SECOND GRID REF DESCRIBES THE END OF THE SEMI-MAJOR AXIS; AND THE THIRD REF DESCRIBES THE END OF THE SEMI-MINOR AXIS OF THE ELLIPSE.

LINE

LINE

SEQUENCE OF GRID REF DESCRIBES A LINE FROM REAR TO FRONT AND FROM LEFT TO RIGHT FACING THE FRONT.

POINT

POINT

EACH GRID REF IS DISPLAYED AS A DOT.

SECTOR

SECTOR

THE FIRST REF DESCRIBES THE LEFT FORWARD EXTREMITY OF THE SECTOR, THE SECOND REF DESCRIBES THE CENTRE FORWARD, AND THE THIRD REF DESCRIBES THE RIGHT FORWARD EXTREMITY OF THE SECTOR.

 

Table 1237/2 - TYPE OF AIRSPACE SHAPE (4-8)

 

Related Documents:

Sponsor:

Unit of Measure:

 

 

TYPE OF AIRSPACE SHAPE (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

CIRCLE

CIRCLE

None

CORRIDOR

CORRIDOR

A SERIES OF POINTS WITH A EQUAL WIDTH EITHER SIDE OF CENTER LINE.

LINE

LINE

SEQUENCE OF GRID REF DESCRIBES A LINE FROM REAR TO FRONT AND FROM LEFT TO RIGHT FACING THE FRONT.

ORBIT

ORBIT

THE ORBIT IS MAINTAINED WITH EITHER LEFT OR RIGHT TURNS TO STAY WITHIN THE SPACE ALLOTTED.

POINT

POINT

EACH GRID REF IS DISPLAYED AS A DOT.

POLYARC

POLYARC

THE FIRST POINT IS THE POINT OF ORIGIN FOLLOWED BY A RADIUS WITH A BEGINNING AND ENDING RADIAL. THIS FORMS THE ARC. THE POLYGON PORTION IS DRAWN WITH A SEQUENCE OF POINTS THAT ARE TO BE PLOTTED CLOCKWISE.

POLYGON

POLYGON

A POLYGON CONTAINS AT LEAST THREE SIDES AND DRAWN CLOCKWISE. THE FIRST POINT AND THE LAST POINT ARE THE SAME. THE LAST POINT MAY BE OMITTED BUT IS UNDERSTOOD TO ENCLOSE THE SHAPE.

RADARC

RADARC

THE FIRST POINT IS THE POINT OF ORIGIN FOLLOWED BY AN INNER RADIUS (MAY BE EQUAL TO "0") AND AN OUTER RADIUS.

TRACK

TRACK

SIMILAR TO A CORRIDOR EXCEPT THE WIDTH CAN VARY ON EITHER SIDE OF CENTER LINE AND CAN HAVE A SEPARATE ALTITUDE/ALTITUDE BLOCK.
