TASK GROUPING CATEGORY (Data Item)

Data Code

Explanation

AIR-TO-AIR REFUELLING

AAR

(AJP 3.3) THE OBJECTIVE OF AAR OPERATIONS IS TO ENHANCE COMBAT EFFECTIVENESS BY EXTENDING THE RANGE, PAYLOAD OR ENDURANCE OF RECEIVER AIRCRAFT.

COMBAT SEARCH AND RESCUE

CSAR

(AJP 3.3.) CSAR IS A COORDINATED OPERATION USING PRE-ESTABLISHED PROCEDURES FOR THE DETECTION, LOCATION, IDENTIFICATION AND RESCUE OF DOWNED AIRCREW IN HOSTILE TERRITORY IN CRISIS OR WARTIME AND, WHEN APPROPRIATE, OF ISOLATED PERSONNEL IN DISTRESS, WHO ARE TRAINED AND EQUIPPED TO BE RESCUED.

AIR INTERDICTION

AI

(AJP 3.3) (AAP-6) AI IS CONDUCTED TO DESTROY, NEUTRALIZE OR DELAY THE ENEMY'S MILITARY POTENTIAL BEFORE IT CAN BE BROUGHT TO BEAR EFFECTIVELY AGAINST FRIENDLY FORCES AT SUCH DISTANCE FROM FRIENDLY FORCES THAT DETAILED INTEGRATION OF EACH AIR MISSION WITH THE FIRE AND MOVEMENT OF FRIENDLY FORCES IS NOT REQUIRED.

AIR SURVEILLANCE

AIR SURV

(AJP 3.3)  (AAP-6) THE SYSTEMATIC OBSERVATION OF AIRSPACE BY ELECTRONIC, VISUAL OR OTHER MEANS, PRIMARILY FOR THE PURPOSE OF IDENTIFYING AND

DETERMINING THE MOVEMENTS OF AIRCRAFT AND MISSILES, FRIENDLY AND ENEMY, IN THE AIR SPACE UNDER OBSERVATION.

OFFENSIVE COUNTER AIR

OCA

(AJP 3.3) OCA INVOLVES DESTRUCTION OR NEUTRALIZATION OF ENEMY FORCES MOSTLY RANGING THROUGHOUT ENEMY TERRITORY AND GENERALLY CONDUCTED AT THE INITIATIVE OF THE FRIENDLY FORCE. OCA MAY INCLUDE FIGHTER SWEEP, FIGHTER ESCORT, AIRFIELD ATTACK AND ATTACKS AGAINST MISSILES AND ASSOCIATED SYSTEMS ON THE GROUND, INCLUDING SEAD.

AIRBORNE EARLY WARNING

AEW

(AJP 3.3) AEW&C AIRCRAFT ARE SPECIALLY EQUIPPED AND MANNED BY TRAINED CONTROL AND CO-ORDINATION PERSONNEL.  THEY CONTRIBUTE TO THE COMMAND AND CONTROL OF MANY DIFFERENT TYPES OF AIR OPERATION.

DEFENSIVE COUNTER AIR

DCA

(AJP 3.3) DCA INVOLVES DESTRUCTION OR NEUTRALIZATION OF ENEMY FORCES CONDUCTED NEAR TO, OR OVER, FRIENDLY TERRITORY AND ARE GENERALLY REACTIVE TO THE INITIATIVE OF ENEMY AIR ASSETS. DCA INCLUDES THE EMPLOYMENT OF ACTIVE AIR DEFENCE WEAPON SYSTEMS SUCH AS FIGHTERS, SURFACE-TO-AIR MISSILES AND ANTI-AIRCRAFT ARTILLERY, COMPLEMENTED BY PASSIVE DEFENCE MEASURES.

AIR RECONNAISSANCE

AIR RECCE

(AJP 3.3) THE COLLECTION OF INFORMATION OF INTELLIGENCE INTEREST EITHER BY VISUAL OBSERVATION FROM THE AIR OR THROUGH THE USE OF AIRBORNE SENSORS. (AAP-6)

STRATEGIC ATTACK

SA

(AJP 3.3) STRATEGIC ATTACK IS CARRIED OUT TO DEFEAT AN ADVERSARY'S CENTRE OF GRAVITY OR ATTACK OTHER VITAL TARGET SETS INCLUDING COMMAND ELEMENTS, ENERGY SOURCES, VITAL WAR PRODUCTION ASSETS AND KEY SUPPORTING INFRASTRUCTURE TO ACHIEVE THE DESIRED EFFECTS. IT IS NORMALLY CONDUCTED BEYOND THE RANGE OF SURFACE FORCE ASSETS.

AIR TRANSPORT

AT

(AJP 3.3) AIR TRANSPORT (AT), COMMONLY KNOWN AS AIRLIFT, PROVIDES A MILITARY COMMANDER WITH THE CAPABILITY TO DEPLOY, EMPLOY AND REDEPLOY FORCES AND EQUIPMENT QUICKLY AND OVER CONSIDERABLE DISTANCES, SUSTAIN THOSE FORCES AND SUPPORT EFFECTIVE APPLICATION OF THEIR MILITARY EFFORT. AT OPERATIONS ARE CONSIDERED IN TWO CATEGORIES: STRATEGIC (INTER-THEATRE) AND THEATRE (INTRA-THEATRE).

CLOSE AIR SUPPORT

CAS

(AJP 3.3) (AAP-6) CAS IS DEFINED AS AIR ACTION AGAINST HOSTILE TARGETS WHICH ARE IN CLOSE PROXIMITY TO FRIENDLY FORCES AND WHICH REQUIRE DETAILED INTEGRATION OF EACH AIR MISSION WITH THE FIRE AND MOVEMENT OF THOSE FORCES.

COMBINED AIR OPERATIONS

COMAO

COMAOS ARE DESIGNED TO MAXIMIZE THE IMPACT OF A GIVEN FORCE BY CONCENTRATING ITS EFFECT, WHILST AT THE SAME TIME MINIMIZING ATTRITION THROUGH MUTUAL SUPPORT WITHIN THE ATTACKING FORCE AND BY SATURATING DEFENCES EN-ROUTE, AND IN THE TARGET AREA. TO ACHIEVE ITS AIM, A COMAO MAY NEED AAR, AD, RECONNAISSANCE, SURVEILLANCE, TARGETING DESIGNATION, JAMMING AND DEFENCE SUPPRESSION AIRCRAFT IN SUPPORT.

AIRBORNE COMMAND AND CONTROL

ABCC

(AJP 3.3) ABCC AIRCRAFT ARE SPECIALLY EQUIPPED AND MANNED BY TRAINED CONTROL AND CO-ORDINATION PERSONNEL.  THEY CONTRIBUTE TO THE COMMAND AND CONTROL OF MANY DIFFERENT TYPES OF AIR OPERATION.

ELECTRONIC WARFARE

EW

(AJP 3.3) EW IS THE EMPLOYMENT OF THE FULL RANGE OF MODERN COMMUNICATIONS, SURVEILLANCE AND WEAPON SYSTEMS OPERATING ACROSS THE ELECTROMAGNETIC SPECTRUM, AND IN AN ATTEMPT TO DOMINATE THE SPECTRUM, WHILST DENYING ITS USE TO THE ENEMY.

ANTI SUBMARINE WARFARE

ASW

(AJP 3.3) THE AIM OF ASW IS TO DENY THE ADVERSARY EFFECTIVE USE OF SUBMARINES. COUNTERING THE SUBMARINE THREAT DEMANDS AN EXTENSIVE RANGE OF CAPABILITIES AND MAY INVOLVE THE USE OF FIXED-WING MPA, HELICOPTERS, FRIENDLY SUBMARINES, SURFACE SHIPS OR OTHER AIRCRAFT.

AIR DEFENCE INTERCEPTION

INTERCEPT

AN INTERCEPT MISSION INVOLVES THE USE OF AN AIRCRAFT TO INTERCEPT ENEMY OR UNKNOWN AIRCRAFT. UPON COMPLETION OF THE INTERCEPT OTHER ACTIONS MAY BE ORDERED E.G. VISUAL IDENTIFICATION (INTERROGATION), SHADOWING, DIVERSION OF THE TARGET FROM ITS INTENDED FLIGHT PATH OR MISSION (INTERVENTION) OR ENGAGEMENT.

ANTI SURFACE WARFARE

ASUW

(AJP 3.3) THE AIM OF ASUW IS TO PREVENT AN ADVERSARY FROM EFFECTIVELY EMPLOYING HIS SURFACE FORCES. AIRCRAFT, SUBMARINES OR SURFACE VESSELS CAN CARRY OUT THIS TASK, PREFERABLY IN A COORDINATED ACTION. ASUW COVERS A WIDE RANGE OF OPERATIONS INVOLVING SURVEILLANCE AND RECONNAISSANCE MISSIONS THAT MAY CULMINATE IN THE TARGETING AND ATTACK OF ENEMY VESSELS.

AIR SURVEILLANCE AND CONTROL SYSTEM

ASACS

(AJP 3.3) A NETWORK OF FIXED, MOBILE AND AIRBORNE RADARS, ASSOCIATED COMMUNICATIONS AND FACILITIES THAT PROVIDE FOR THE DETECTION, RECOGNITION, REPORTING AND CONTROL OF INTERCEPTION AND ENGAGEMENT OF AIRBORNE VEHICLES WITHIN THE DETECTION RANGE.

SPECIAL AIR OPERATIONS

SAO

(AJP 3.3) SAO SUPPORT SPECIAL OPERATIONS AND MAY BE CONDUCTED DURING PEACE, CRISIS AND CONFLICT. SAO ACTIVITIES MAY INCLUDE BOTH COMBAT AND NON-COMBAT TASKS INVOLVING THE DEPLOYMENT, INFILTRATION, SUPPORT, EXFILTRATION AND WITHDRAWAL OF SPECIAL FORCES.  SAO MAY BE CONDUCTED IN CONJUNCTION WITH OTHER JOINT OPERATIONS OR MAY BE AUTONOMOUS.

AIRBORNE OPERATIONS

AO

(AJP 3.3) AIR DELIVERED COMBAT POWER CAN BE USED TO SEIZE GROUND OR INSTALLATIONS THAT ARE VITAL TO THE ENEMY BY DELIVERING LAND FORCES DIRECTLY ONTO THE OBJECTIVE. THIS MAY BE ACHIEVED BY PARACHUTE INSERTION (AIRBORNE) OR TACTICAL AIR LANDED OPERATION (TALO). PORTS OF ENTRY, AIRFIELDS, AND KEY INSTALLATIONS OR STRUCTURES, ALL OFFER POTENTIAL OBJECTIVES.

PACKAGE

PACKAGE

FORCE PACKAGING IS USED IF THE REQUIREMENT IS TO CONCENTRATE A LARGE COMPOSITE FORCE AGAINST A SINGLE OBJECTIVE, AND PARTICULARLY IF THERE IS A NEED FOR COMBAT SUPPORT FORCES. THE OPTIMUM COMBINATION OF IMPACT AND SURVIVABILITY MAY ONLY BE ACHIEVED IF THE FORCE OPERATES AS AN INTEGRATED UNIT.

AMPHIBIOUS OPS SUPPORT

AMB SUP

(AJP 3.3) AIR SUPPORT FOR AMPHIBIOUS OPERATIONS CAN BE PROVIDED BY BOTH LAND AND SEA BASED AIR ASSETS DEPENDING UPON THE LOCATION OF THE AMPHIBIOUS OBJECTIVE AREA (AOA). AIR SUPPORT WILL NORMALLY INCLUDE THE AI AND CAS ROLES OF AIR-LAND OPERATIONS, BUT MAY ALSO REQUIRE COUNTER-AIR AND SUPPORTING AIR OPERATIONS.

SEARCH AND RESCUE

SAR

(AJP 3.3) SAR IS DEFINED AS THE USE OF AIRCRAFT, SURFACE CRAFT,

SUBMARINES, SPECIALIZED RESCUE TEAMS AND EQUIPMENT TO SEARCH FOR AND RESCUE PERSONNEL IN DISTRESS ON LAND OR AT SEA (AAP-6).

SURFACE-TO-AIR WEAPONS

SAW

SAW, COMPRISING SURFACE-TO-AIR MISSILES AND ANTI-AIRCRAFT ARTILLERY, IS AN INTEGRAL PART OF A DCA MISSION.

UNIT TASKING

UNIT

UNIT TASKING PROVIDES THE CAPABILITY TO TASK ALL MISSIONS OF ONE UNIT IN SINGLE SECTION OF THE ATO MISSION FILE.  THIS CAPABILITY ALSO PROVIDES MULTI-ROLE AIRCRAFT UNITS A SINGLE SECTION OF THE ATO FOR THEIR UNIT MISSION TASKING.
